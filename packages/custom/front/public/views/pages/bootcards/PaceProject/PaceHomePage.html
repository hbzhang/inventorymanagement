INSTRUCTIONS

This tool allows faculty and supervisors to plan annual performance and professional development goals. It uses ACPA/NASPA competencies and industry-specific competencies related to the faculty member’s field of expertise. Additional faculty performance management resources are available at the DSA HR SharePoint site, which can be accessed from the departments’ navigation on Your DSA Home. The following are the phases for completing what is referred to as the PACE process.

 

  1. Planning The shaded portions of Sections A, B, and C are completed by the faculty member at the beginning of the performance cycle to establish goals and professional development plans. (Note: For new faculty, this should be completed approximately 30 days after their start date.) Goals should support departmental and Division of Student Affairs’ strategic goals.
  2. Affirmation The faculty member and the faculty member’s supervisor meet to review the performance plan and negotiate any changes, expectations, and/or resources needed to achieve the plan in Sections A, B, and C. Signatures should be completed in Section D.
  3. Communication The faculty member and their supervisor should maintain communication regarding the progress and/or needed changes to performance goals and the professional development plan throughout the year.
  4. Evaluation This phase is completed in five steps at the end of the performance cycle and includes an overall review of the faculty member’s accomplishments and progress towards the selected goals.
    1. The faculty member completes the self-evaluation in the un-shaded blocks with an asterisk (*) of Sections A, B, C, and E. The faculty member then passes the self-evaluation to the faculty member’s supervisor.
    2. The supervisor reviews the self-evaluation and makes comments on Sections A, B, C, and E. The supervisor then schedules a meeting with the faculty member to discuss the evaluation.
    3. The faculty member and supervisor review Sections A, B, C, and E and then complete Sections F and G together during the meeting. The supervisor then passes the completed evaluation to the supervisor’s supervisor (the reviewer) for review.
    4. The reviewer examines Section A, B, C, E, and F, makes comments in Section E, and signs in Section G. The reviewer then passes the completed evaluation back to the supervisor.
    5. The supervisor notifies DSA Human Resources of the overall performance rating and files the evaluation tool in the department’s personnel file.

Rating Definitions for Goals/Responsibilities:

Exceeds Expectations Strong Needs Improvement Unsatisfactory
Outstanding performance that considerably and consistently exceeds expectations. Accomplishments were made in unexpected areas. Solid performance. Consistently meets expectations and at times, exceeds expectations. Goals were achieved at, or at times, more than established standards. Partially meets performance expectations but needs improvement. Improvement is achievable. Requires greater than usual time and attention by supervisor. Performance is consistently below expectations. Requires significant time and attention by the supervisor.
